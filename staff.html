Phillip Morrow, SoBRO's President/CEO

Phillip Morrow joined the South Bronx Overall Economic Development Corporation (SoBRO) as President in January of 1996. Mr. Morrow brought to SoBRO over twenty years of experience in the community-economic development field, with particular expertise in real estate development. In his former role as Director of Development at the Harlem Community Development Corporation, he launched and managed successful housing and economic development programs ranging from the reopening of the Apollo Theater to the construction of Harlemâ€™s first moderately priced condominiums.

In his earlier career, Mr. Morrow launched commercial, housing and job training initiatives for the City of Hartford, CT that included development of over 300 low income housing units. Between 1979 and 1981, Mr. Morrow served as Director of Public-Private Partnerships for the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) in Washington, D.C. where he developed national models for neighborhood development that encouraged private sector investment in underserved communities. Throughout his career, he has overseen development of over 5,000 units of new and renovated housing. Under Mr. Morrow's leadership, SoBRO's service to the South Bronx community has expanded to include development of hundreds of new and renovated housing for low and moderate-income families.

Sharon Higgins, Project Manager

Sharon Higgins is a results-oriented financial professional with demonstrated expertise in financial analysis, commercial financing, and risk management. With over seven years of commercial lending and underwriting experience with micro loans financing and with the United States Small Business Administration (SBA) 504 Loan programs. She has made extensive contribution in the implementation and facilitation of several multi-million dollar federal, state, and city economic development small business expansion projects. Her work experience includes working in various areas of financing, business development, reporting and implementation processes.

Sharon is also an experienced higher education administration professional, with experience in academic advising at graduate level. She holds a Master of Public Administration, Master of Science in Higher Education Administration, Bachelor in Accounting, and Associate of Applied Science in Business Administration.

Jason Elijio, Business Specialist

Jason Elijio is a graduate of St. John's University where he earned his Bachelor of Arts in History focusing on Classics and European colonialism in the Middle East and Africa. Mr. Elijio completed his graduate study at the New York Institute of Technology, earning a Master of Science specializing in Energy Management from the College of Engineering and Computing Sciences.

Mr. Elijio was previously employed as an Associate with Bill Lynch Associates LLC, primarily working in the areas of campaign consultation and strategic analysis. Prior to this, he worked as the Research Coordinator and Assistant Managing Editor for the World Energy Forum, a non-governmental organization which facilitated analysis and dialogue between global shareholders on issues of sustainable development and energy utilization, providing qualitative and technical expertise on energy policy and technology as needed. He is also the Economics and Foreign Policy Correspondent for Let Your Voice Be Heard Radio on WHCR-FM, 90.3 FM.

Andrew Hyman, Business Specialist

Andrew Hyman has nearly 35 years of experience and expertise in economic and community development, and real estate law. Born in Brooklyn, New York, Mr. Hyman has a Bachelor's Degree from Vassar College and a law degree from Albany Law School of Union University.

Andrew began his career as an Assistant Counsel with the Lefrak Organization, a major residential and commercial real estate developer. He then gained experience in affordable housing issues as a Senior Attorney with the New York State Division of Housing and Community Renewal, and in commercial lending as Senior Counsel with the Empire State Development Corporation.

Andrew served as General Counsel to Banana Kelly Community Improvement Association, and as Deputy Director of the Bronx Empowerment Zone Unit with the Bronx Overall Economic Development Corporation ("BOEDC"). He was promoted to the position of General Counsel for the BOEDC.

Most recently, Mr. Hyman served as Commissioner of Public Safety and Deputy Town Attorney in his home community of the Town of North Hempstead. He retired from state and local service in October 2012. Mr. Hyman is married to the former Corbey Rene Low; they have three children-Doriana, age 19, Ezra, age 17, and Mikayla, age 15.

Now, as a Business Specialist with the Minority Business Center, operated by South Bronx Overall Economic Development Corporation, Andrew looks forward to serving the Bronx business community.

Ibrahima Souare, Administrative Coordinator

Ibrahima Souare is a native of the West African country Guinea. He immigrated to the United States of America at the age of seven. Mr. Souare, being from a francophone country, speaks three additional languages aside from English, which are French, Susu and Manike.
